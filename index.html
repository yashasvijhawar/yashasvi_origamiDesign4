    • Interesting Facts about Camel

    • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
    • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
    • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

    Interseting Facts about Chameleon

    • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the Iguana suborder.
    • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
    • Chameleons actual eyesight is great they can see small insects 5-10 meters away.

    Interesting Facts about Pigeons

    • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
    • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
    • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.

    Interesting Facts about Cicada

    • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar
    • Their enemies are the stuff of nightmares
    • Some cicadas are really tiny

    Interesting Facts about Teddy bears

    • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
    • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
    • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

    Interesting Facts about Pandas

    • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
    • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
    • Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.
